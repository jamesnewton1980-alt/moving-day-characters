Page 1"There, Mom... I have finished packing my last box," said Ida proudly. "Great, Eeedah!" Mom said, laughing.
Page 2Ida was sad since the family was moving to Toronto, Canada, and Ida would be leaving her good friends behind.
Page 3Her Mom had said it would be a new adventure for all. Her Dad had rented a house near a very large park with a playground and trails for walking.
Page 4From a corner of Ida's room came a soft sobbing sound. It was Bippy, a longtime friend. Tears were running down his cheek, and he was shaking.
Page 5Ida gently picked him up and, in a soft voice, said, "Don't cry, Bippy. It will be all right."
Page 6Bippy said, "I don't want to leave my friends, my school, and my house. It is very scary, and what will life be like in Canada?"
Page 7Ida held him close, and finally Bippy stopped crying. "It will be okay," she said. "Mom and Dad, CC, and I will be there to keep you safe."
Page 8Bippy felt more comfortable as Ida held his hand, but he still was sad.
Page 9Soon it was bedtime. They washed, brushed their teeth, put on pajamas, and climbed into bed.
Page 10Tomorrow morning they would all be heading to Toronto. Ida was anxious and sad but looked forward to the new home.
Page 11She had spoken with her good friend Reid and made plans to speak on FaceTime. Bippy was sound asleep with a big smile on his face.
Page 12Ida pulled the covers tight and drifted into a deep sleep. "Good morning, chipmunks. Time to get up. We are leaving in a few minutes," Dad said, smiling.
Page 13"Ida," Mom called, "why don't you get Bippy and his little suitcase into the car. Be sure to take your favorite pillow and blanket."
Page 14Finally, they were on their way. Ida looked back at their house one more time, and Bippy started to cry. CC whimpered.
Page 15Ida pulled the blanket tightly around them all, creating a warm, cozy nest.
Page 16Once on the highway, Bippy was looking out the window and chattering away about the beautiful scenery.
Page 17Ida laughed, and soon both of them were once again under the blanket. Then Bippy looked sad and began to cry.
Page 18"I don't want to leave! I want my friends," he sobbed. "I am scared of going to a strange house and a strange land!"
Page 19"You will be fine. We are all together, and it will be a great adventure," said Ida as she held him closely. Bippy felt a little better and managed a weak smile.
Page 20"Okay, I will try." Mom called back to them, "We are at the border!"
Page 21A Canadian border guard appeared, smiling, and said, "Welcome to Canada! But first I have to check your car and yourselves."
Page 22After talking to Mom and completing his inspection, he waved them across, and they were on their way.
Page 23The road passed by the big city of Toronto. Bippy and Ida were amazed at the gleaming skyscrapers. It looked like a crystal city.
Page 24Mom pointed to other areas and said, "Over there is a beautiful aquarium. Next to it is the baseball stadium, and that spire is a tower from the World Fair."
Page 25Bippy and Ida were excited and were looking forward to visiting the aquarium. By the time they arrived at their new home, it was mid-afternoon.
Page 26Dad was waiting with two men who were unloading the truck. Bippy looked up and said, "This looks just like our old home!" "Yes!" shouted Ida.
Page 27"It has a porch, a yard, and look over there!" There, stretching as far as he could see, was a big park.
Page 28Inside the house, the rooms were a bit smaller than their old home but familiar. Ida and Bippy ran upstairs to see their bedroom.
Page 29Dad had placed all of their furniture into the room. "Look," they said to each other, "it is exactly like our old room!"
Page 30Mom came upstairs, and Dad followed. Seeing them, Ida and Bippy gave them a big hug, saying, "Thank you."
Page 31It was a little before dinnertime when an unexpected thunderstorm appeared and just as quickly disappeared.
Page 32Ida grabbed Bippy by the hand and said, "Let's go see the yard!" CC barked in agreement, and they all ran to the back door.
Page 33The three of them walked down three steps into the grassy yard. Ida and Bippy stopped short and let out a loud, "WOW!"
Page 34"Oh my goodness!" said Ida. "It is beautiful!" Bippy shouted. CC even stopped and looked up.
Page 35There in the east was a huge, beautifully formed, brightly colored double rainbow arching across the sky.
Page 36Bippy and Ida held hands. CC sat in front of them. Mom and Dad stood behind them, smiling. They all knew that this was now their new home, and it would be wonderful.
